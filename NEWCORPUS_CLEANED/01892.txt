There  is  one  more  tag  they  hadn't  noticed  flapping  behind  her.  Stephanie  says,  &quot;Do  you  think  Great-Grandma  gets  embarrassed  when  she  says  things  wrong?&quot;  She  has  to  shout  to  be  heard  over  the  sound  of  the  engines  and  the  wind.  &quot;I  don't  know!&quot;  he  shouts  back.  &quot;I  THINK-MAYBE-NOT.&quot;    &quot;When  I'm  in  my  nineties  I'm  not  going  to  say  anything-just  to  make  sure,&quot;  Stephanie  says  loudly.  She  spreads  her  arms.  &quot;I'm  blowing  away!&quot;  She  runs  from  Jack  to  the  railing  on  the  opposite  side  of  the  deck  and  then  full  tilt-almost  full  tilt-  straight  at  him,  and  throws  herself  against  him.  Her  hair  feels  soft  and  tangled  on  his  chest  but  her  head  is  hard.  He  knows  he's  not  supposed  to  put  his  arm  around  her-it's  a  game.  Sure  enough,  she  is  bouncing  off  him  to  run  back  across  the  deck.  Then  again  she  runs  into  him.  She  is  pre-  tending  to  be  helpless  in  the  wind  and  under  the  force  of  her  own  energy,  but  he  can  tell  by  the  feel  of  her  landing  that  she  isn't.  There's  just  a  little  control  at  the  last  second,  a  little  re-  straint.  How  many  of  us,  he  asks  him-  self,  can  just  let  go?  "
