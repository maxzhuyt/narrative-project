On  its  dull-green  walls,  a  mockery  of  the  grass  and  green  leaves  of  life,  the  sun  never  shines.  All  day  and  all  night,  the  big  windows  at  the  ends  of  the  corridor,  one  at  the  east  wing  of  the  house  and  one  at  the  west,  are  wide  open,  and  from  whichever  airt  the  wind  does  blow  it  always  blows  cold.
