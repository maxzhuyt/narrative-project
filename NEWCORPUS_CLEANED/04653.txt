He  uprooted  the  field  flow-  ers  Medley  had  brought  from  the  Hud-  son  [REDACTED],  not  in  anger  but  because  they  had  never  really  belonged  with  the  rest  of  the  planting,  and  just  as  he  threw  them  into  the  lily  pond,  Blossom  Duveen  drove  up.  &quot;Came  by  to  remind  you  it's  bridge  night,&quot;  she  called  out  in  her  vulgar,  brassy  voice.  &quot;No  goulashes  tonight,  I  hope,  I  hope.&quot;  As  he  strolled  over  totalkto  her,  Dr.  Bohrmann  listed  to  himself  some  of  the  other  pleasures  of  life:  this  dumb  dear,  for  example,  after  Medley  with  his  hellbent  enlightenment;  bridge  after  ombre;  a  contented  Mrs.  Pritchard.  &quot;What  gives?&quot;  said  Miss  Duveen.  &quot;You  look  like  the  cat  that  swallowed  the  canary.&quot;  &quot;I  am,&quot;  said  Dr.  Bohrmann,  grin-  ning  conspiratorially  at  Grimalkin  who  was  washing  thetop  of  his  brainy  head.
