Okoab,  in  the  Kalahari  Desert  of  [REDACTED]  Africa,  was  a  trading  store  and  an  iron  shed,  a  windmill  and  water  tank,  two  houses  and  a  few  huts. Not  far  from  his  shelter  were  the  tents  belonging  to  the  leader  of  the  prospecting  team.  He  was  a  white  man,  a  geologist  from  Johannesburg  hired  to  search  for  diamond-bearing  rock  in  the  desert.  Kleinba'  &quot;the  young  chief  &quot;-was  what  Pitjie  called  him.  In  one  of  the  tents  was  Jonkman.  He  was  dead.  They  had  carried  him  there  and  laid  him  down,  and  he  died  quietly;  he  bled  at  first  from  the  wound,  but  not  much  after  he  had  been  bandaged.  Pitjie  had  killed  him.  He  did  it  in  a  passion,  and  now  he  was  subdued  and  not  affected  anymore.  At  least,  he  did  not  show  either  that  he  was  glad  or  that  he  regretted  it.  He  showed  noth-  ing,  and  his  face  was  dark  and  unchangingunder  the  hanging  brim  of  his  hat.  The  police  did  not  know  yet.  They  had  still  to  be  in-  formed,  and  then  they  would  come  for  him,  that  was  sure.  No  one  stood  guard  over  him.  He  was  not  running  away. One  man's  anger  was  another's  death.  Kleinba'  had  left  him  behind  there  as  if  he,too,  no  longer  lived.  The  dagbreckertjie,  the  little  fly-  catcher,  is  making  its  whistling  song  for  the  coming  day.  It  is  unbearably  gay;  to  it  every  day  is  the  same.  Pitjie  listens,  but  he  cannot  see  the  bird  from  where  he  sits  in  the  dust  of  his  shelter.  The  birds  speak  about  the  earth  and  the  sky  and  the  wind.  They  also  speak  about  the  rain,  which  comes  seldom  in  the  desert.  The  bird  flies  across  the  opening  of  the  shelter  and  is  again  out  of  sight. What  he  was  doing  Pitjie  could  not  see  from  where  he  stood  some  way  off,  silent,  unseen,  the  color  of  the  scrub  and  the  veld,  wearing  the  rags  of  an  old  coat  which  had  turned  reddish  like  the  dust.
