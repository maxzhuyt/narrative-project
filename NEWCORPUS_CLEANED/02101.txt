I  am  quite  pleased  with  it-it  has  no  supports  on  one  side,  and  its  simplicity  lends  it  a  certain  elegance.  Then  the  building  inspector  comes  and  approves  all  the  work.  At  last,  everything  is  done.  It  has  cost  me  more  than  I  imagined-  ten  or  twelve  thousand  dollars;  I've  lost  count.  But  through  the  win-  dows  I  can  see  a  pond,  crosshatched  by  trees,  and  birds  flying,  and  I  can  hear  their  song.  The  sun  shines  through  the  skylight,  and  at  night  the  moon  and  the  stars.  It  is  much  more  peace-  ful  here  than  out  front.  The  atmosphere  seems  just  right  for  a  studio,  right  for  thought;  in  the  proper  mood,  what  bright  work  might  it  cradle,  if  not  in  me,  then  in  others-my  children,  or  theirs.
