 b"IN  my  tours  around  the  nation  I  am  I  frequently  asked,  &quot;Have  you  ever  broken  a  cup?&quot;  Of  course  I  have.  Don't  let  anybody  kid  you  on  that  score.  Everyone  who  regularly  drinks  from  china,  no  matter  how  adept  he  has  be-  come,  has  had  his  share  of  ruined  table-  cloths  and  scalded  knees.  No  Auman  being  is  bornwith  the  ability  to  take  liquid  from  a  cup  successfully;  you  can  easily  prove  this  by  trying  to  feed  a  baby.  Those  of  us  who  have  attained  some  proficiency  have  done  so  at  the  price  of  long  hours  of  systematic  application.  Without  these  long  hours  our  natural  grace  and  poise  would  neverhave  evolved  into  skill.  I  would  not  say  that  everyone  is  endowed  equally;  I  do  say  that  everyone,  no  matter  how  clumsy,  can  reduce  his  accidents  to  a  minimum  that  will  amaze  his  wife  and  friends.  He  can  do  this  by  rigorously  adhering  to  a  few  simple  principles  that  I  have  discov-  ered  through  painful  trial-and-error.  Had  these  principles  been  available  in  legible  form  when  I  was  young,  my  present  eminence  would  have  been  at-  tained  by  me  years  ago.  I  have  analyzed  drinking  from  a  cup  into  three  three-part  stages:  (1)  Re-  ceipt,  (2)  The  Cooling  Pause,  and  (3)  Consummation.  However,  bear  in  mind  that  in  practice  these  &quot;compartments&quot;  are  run  together  in  one  fluid,  harmo-  nious  social  action.  I. What  is  it  doing  there?
