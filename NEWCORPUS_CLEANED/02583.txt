We  are  not  Catholics,  but  Alexander  Ross  goes  to  a  Catholic  boys'  school  for  our  mother's  sake.  She  wanted  him  to  go  to  the  school  his  father  went  to. A  car  out  by  the  fishing  shacks  fell  through  the  ice.  Alexander  Ross  and  my  father  did  not  see  it  fall;  they  had  already  started  walking  away,  back  to  shore,  carrying  five  rabbits  that  dangled  from  a  pole.  I  stood  by  myself,  watching  while  the  car  settled  into  the  water,  floating  and  sinking  slowly  on  the  ice  broken  away  beneath  it.  I  did  not  call  them  back  to  see.
