 b"WASHINGTON,  May  8-The  Supreme  Court  took  the  fol-  lowing  actions  today:  FIRST  AMENDMENT  N  a  landmark  decision,  the  Court  ruled  unanimously  in  favor  of  a  twelve-year-old  plaintiff  who  sought  damages  on  account  of  being  denied  the  chance  to  audition  for  the  Clint  Eastwood  rolein  the  motion  picture  &quot;Maddened  Rustlers.&quot;  The  Court's  opinion,  written  by  Chief  Justice  Happ,  argued  that  exclusion  of  the  little  girl  was  &quot;rotten,  beastly,  a  crying  shame-  really  makes  the  Court  sick.&quot;  The  case  was  not  decided,  as  had  been  expected,  on  the  ground  of  sex  discrimination;  rather,  the  Justices  invoked  the  First  Amendment's  guarantee  of  freedom  of  expression.  The  Court  thus  affirmed  for  the  first  time  the  constitutional  right  to  a  screen  test.  SEARCH  AND  SEIZURE  OVERTURNING  the  &quot;dog's  break-  fast&quot;  doctrine  of  search  and  sei-  zure,  the  Court  held  unconstitutional  the  Drug  Enforcement  Administra-  tion's  system  of  obtaining  search  war-  rants,  under  which  a  judge  who  issues  a  warrant  receives  the  rest  of  the  after-  noon  off,  while  a  judge  who  refuses  a  warrant  is  reclassified  as  a  Controlled  Substance.  Justice  Happsberger,  writ-  ing  for  the  majority,  said  that  such  pro-  cedures  &quot;lean  upon  the  delicately  coiffed  maiden  of  the  Fourth  Amend-  ment  with  the  great  ugly  brutish  heav-  ily  muscled  shoulder  of  procedural  er-  ror,&quot;  and  cited  Judge  Cheerful  Hand's  famous  dictum  &quot;I  shall  keep  at  it  with  these  metaphors  until  I'm  very  old  and  it's  unbecoming.&quot;  TAXES  \xe2\x80\x98N? In  the  majority  opinion,  Chief  Justice  Happ  explained  that  appellant's  argu-  ments  were  &quot;unprepossessing  and-  let's  be  frank  about  it-just  incredi-  bly  disingenuous.&quot;  Dissenting,  Justices  Happer  and  Happner  said  they  wanted  to  pretend  to  hear  the  case  and  then  rule  against  appellant  for  &quot;putting  out  sucha  clumsily  edited  and  typographi-  cally  unappetizing  publication.&quot;  In  a  related  decision,  the  Justices  unanimously  refused  to  hear  a  song  written  by  a  Kleagle  of  the  Ku  Klux  Klan.  CRIMINAL  Y  a  9-0  vote,  the  Court  held  un-  constitutional  a  New  York  City  statute  that  would  have  mandated  criminal  convictions  for  suspects  who  fail  to  take  policemen  aside  and  &quot;read  them  their  duties.&quot;  The  statute  had  re-quired  that  suspects  deliver  these  &quot;Cali-  ban  warnings&quot;  to  policemen  in  order  to  remind  them  of  their  power  of  life  and  death,  their  obligation  to  at-  tend  to  personal  hygiene,  etc.  The  Court,  in  an  opinion  by  Justice  Hap-  pell,  contended,  &quot;Who  can  doubt  that  this  would  be  the  first  step  toward  compelling  suspects  to  serve  their  ar-  resting  officers  creamed  chicken  on  toast  points?&quot;  GREED  PLITTING  8-1,  the  Court  up-  held  the  constitutionality  of  a  fed-  eral  program  for  the  redistribution  of  wealth.  Under  the  program,  which  is  known  as  &quot;horizontal  divestiture,&quot;  rich  people  are  asked  to  lie  down,  and  poor  people  then  divest  them  of  their  money.  Justice  Happold,  dissenting,  said  that  the  program  would  diminish  the  impact  of  a  standing  Court  order  requiring  that  income  in  excess  of  $8,000  a  year  be  bused  across  state  lines  to  achieve  bank-account  balances.  GIBBERISH  HE  Court  voted  unanimously  not  to  review  a  case  in  which  a  court  of  appeals  struck  down  a  lower  federal  court's  decision  to  vacate  an  even  lower  court's  refusal  to  uphold  a  ruling  that  it  is  not  unconstitutional  to  practice  &quot;re-  verse  discrimination.&quot;  Chief  Justice  Happ,  who  wrote  the  opinion,  said  that  the  Court  &quot;is  not,  nor  will  it  con-  sent  to  be,  a  body  of  foolosophers  easily  drawn  into  jive  baloney-shooting.&quot;  A  brief  was  filed  by  the  Modern  Lan-  guage  Association  as  amicus  curie  (&quot;curiosity  about  Don  Ameche&quot;).
