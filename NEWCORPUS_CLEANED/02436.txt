The  snow  came  down  in  steady  lines,  but  I  squinted  and  made  out  two  gliding  gray  forms,hardly  visible,  moving  as  if  in  the  severest  of  all  mysteries.  I  dared  not  approach  them,  though  I  could  have.  They  seemed  like  lions  on  the  plain,  or  spirits,  or  frightening  angels.  Then  I  turned  at  the  sound  of  snowshoes  and  saw  my  grandmother  coming  up  the  rise  to  where  I  stood.  When  she  reached  me,  she  put  her  hand  on  my  shoulder  and  looked  hard  at  the  loons.  She,  too,  looked  sleepless.  &quot;I  heard  you,&quot;  she  said,  &quot;when  you  left  the  house.  Do  you  see  them?&quot;  For  reasons  I  could  not  discern,  I  began  to  cry.  She  dropped  to  her  knees,  kneeling  on  her  snowshoes,  and  took  me  in  her  arms.  She  didn't  have  to  say  anything.  For  I  saw  that  her  eyes  ...  her  eyes,  though  beautiful  and  blue,  were  as  cold  as  ice. "
