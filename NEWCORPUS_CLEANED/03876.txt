Schwarz  was  just  across  the  street?&quot;  He  pulled  her  hand  through  his  arm  and  they  walked  on  toward  Fifty-ninth. Their  eyes  had  locked  no  more  than  a  moment  there,  but  for  him  it  had  been  one  of  those  moments  people  are  said  to  have  when  the  parachute  fails  and  a  lifetime  flashes  in  review  as  they  come  down.  Falling  from  a  great  height,  he  plunged  through  his  history.  Girls  names  whispered  like  a  rush  of  wind  in  his  ears,  and  a  blur  of  faces  flitted  by.  Though  they  were  all  different,  the  expression  in  their  eyes  was  the  same.  They  slipped  past  like  a  runaway  reel,  and  when  they  faded  he  came  up  against  the  knowledge-what  she  had  always  known,  of  course-that  it  just  wasn't  in  him.  He  never  had  been,  and  never  would  be,  a  stayer.  Not  for  all  his  playing  house,  his  swelling  heart,  his  pride  in  her  that  prickled  the  nape  of  his  neck  that  very  minute.  And  though  he  loved  her  more  than  ever  for  forgiving  him,  for  playing  out  this  charade  for  his  sake,  he  knew  that  this,  too,  wouldn't  help,  nothing  would  help.  All  the  best  wishes  and  gold  bands  in  the  world  couldn't  change  the  brass  taste  he  had  now  in  his  mouth."
