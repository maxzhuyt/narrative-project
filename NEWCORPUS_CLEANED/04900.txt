For  a  moment,  he  looked  up  at  her  with  so  great  an  anguish  that  she  felt  it  as  if  it  were  she  who  had  received  the  blow.  And  then,  as  quickly,  he  hid  the  look,  turning  away  to  hide  his  quivering  face.  But  her  anger  returned.  In  spite  of  the  expression  she  had  seen-or  per-  hapsbecause  of  it-she  had  to  whip  him  again,  if  onlywith  words.  &quot;How  come  you  had  to  foller  me!&quot;  she  yelled.  &quot;Lit-  tle  ole  sneakin'  skunk-that's  all  you  are!&quot;  With  an  unintelligible  cry  of  de-  fiance,  he  turned  and  ran,  away  from  the  building  and  in  the  direction  oppo-  site  to  where  their  father  had  parked  the  car.  For  a  few  moments,  Shellie  stood  there,  letting  the  heat  bear  down  upon  her  head.  Then  she  walked  slowly  around  the  building,  shaky  on  her  high  heels.  At  the  corner,  with  abrupt  decision,  she  slipped  out  of  her  shoes  and,  picking  them  up,  began  to  run  in  the  direction  Burl  had  taken. "
