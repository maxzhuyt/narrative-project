 b"A recent Associated Press  dispatch  from  Freedom  Village  mentioned  the  fact  that  Allied  officers  in  charge  of  the  prisoner  ex-  change  wore  armbands  with  the  letters  RAMP,  which  were  &quot;believed  to  stand  for  'repatriation  of  allied  military  per-  sonnel.'  &quot;  I  can  appreciate  the  re-  porter's  uncertainty. In  fact,  it  seems  to  be  almost  an  Army  poli-  cy  to  retire  an  abbreviation  as  soon  as  it  is  understood.  This  happened  to  &quot;G1,&quot;  and  since  then  to  &quot;PW.&quot;  The  Army  is  happiest,I  suppose,  when  it  has  ab-  breviations  that  mean  two  or  more  things-such  as  &quot;OD,&quot;  which  stands  for  both  Officer  of  the  Day  and  the  Olive  Drab  he  wears,  and  &quot;CO,&quot;  which  means  Commanding  Officer,  or  the  Company  he  commands,  or  the  Communications  Officer  within  it.
