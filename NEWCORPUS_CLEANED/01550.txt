The  summer  evening  would  have  been  full  of  dinner  smells:  hamburger  patties  dripping  fat  onto  the  hot  briquettes  in  those  old  standup  cookers,  barbecue  sauce  burning,  the  sweet  garlic  smell  of  Hebrew  National  hot  dogs,  and  the  mild  petroleum  vapor  of  charcoal-lighter  fluid.  And  there  would  be  the  usual  neighborhood  sounds,  softened  by  the  absorbent  foliage:  the  piercing  notes  of  chil-  dren,  parents  shouting  from  house  to  house  through  the  screened  win-  dows,  radios  and  televisions  ringing  through  the  forest  late  into  the  day-  light  hours  of  a  summer  night  still  bright  at  seven-thirty.
