Mustn't  let  Mummy  think  you've  been  crying,  must  we?&quot;  I  took  out  my  handker-  chief  and  dabbed  it  on  his  cheeks  and  under  his  glasses.  I  kissed  him  on  the  head  as  I  lifted  him  down.  We  crossed  the  road  by  Beulagh  Terrace,  and  Raymond  opened  the  gate  and  ran  up  to  the  door.  When  I  reached  the  door,  I  wiped  the  choco-  late  from  his  mouth  and  put  my  hand-  kerchief  away  before  I  rang  the  bell.  Raymond  knocked  on  the  door  with  the  metal  rim  of  his  purse.  We  waited  for  a  few  seconds,  and  I  rang  again.  Usually,  Dora  opened  the  side  window  before  I  finished  ringing,  but  there  was  no  sound  from  the  house.  Raymond  kept  on  knocking  at  the  worn  paint  on  the  door.  A  flake  or  two  fell.  I  took  Raymond's  hand  and  walked  with  him  round  to  the  back  door.  The  kitchen  window  was  half  open.  I  put  my  hand  inside  and  lifted  the  catch,  opened  the  window  wide.  I  shouted  in.  &quot;Dora?  We're  back.  Are  you  there?&quot;  I  didn't  wait  for  an  answer,  but  walked  in  by  the  back  door.  Raymond  had  found  his  red  car  on  the  step  where  all  his  other  tovs  were.  I'd  been  in  the  cottage  once  before-years  ago,  in  the  days  when  it  had  lain  empty.  I  knew  that  the  smaller  of  the  two  doors  lead-  ing  from  the  kitchen  gave  access  through  a  passageway  to  the  front  sit-  ting  room.  As  I  opened  it,  I  heard  Dora  call.  &quot;That  you,  Tom?&quot;  &quot;Yes.  Can  I  come  in?  I  did  knock,  but  you  didn't  hear  me.  Have  you  been  having  a  nap?&quot;  I  looked  round  the  doorjamb  into  the  sitting  room.  Dora  was  looking  in  the  mirror.  She  had  her  back  to  me.  She  was  standing  so  close  to  the  mirror  that  she  touched  it  once  or  twice  with  her  knuckles  as  she  combed  her  hair  briskly.  &quot;Wait  out  there  a  minute,  Tom.  I  won't  be  long,&quot;  she  shouted.  I  enjoyed  being  there,  watching  her  while  she  didn't  know  I  was  only  a  few  feet  away.  She  gathered  the  love-  ly  hair  together  into  a  rope  behind  her,  and  slipped  the  Alice  band  along  it,  and  patted  it  into  place  on  the  top  of  her  head.
