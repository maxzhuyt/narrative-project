THE  SALT  AND  PEPPER  SHAKERS  MY  wife  and  I  have  been  meaning  to  buy  a  set  of  salt  and  pepper  shakers  for  the  past  several  years.  We  have  one  set,  which  we  carry  back  and  forth  between  kitchen  and  table.  For  some  reason,  we  have  never  gotten  around  to  buying  the  second  set.  Ap-  parently  it  isn't  quite  important  enough  for  us  to  do  but  is  important  enough  to  make  us  think  we  ought  to  buy  them  at  some  point.  &quot;Where's  the  salt  and  pepper?&quot;  I  am  heard  frequently  to  ask.  &quot;Oh,  they're  in  the  kitchen,&quot;  my  wife  answers  wearily.  At  which  point  our  son  says,  &quot;O.K.,  I  know,&quot;  and  goes  to  get  the  salt  and  pepper.  And  so  we  do  not  remember  to  buy  the  second  set.  If  someone  were  to  come  up  to  us  on  the  street  and  offer  to  giveus  a  set  rightthere  on  the  spot,  we  would  exclaim,  &quot;It's  exactly  what  we  need,  it's  amazing!&quot;  But  no  one  does.  I  went  outside  a  few  minutes  ago  to  check.  Instead  the  streets  were  oc-  cupied  by  very  bland  &quot;people&quot;  with  no  interest  in  giving  me  some  salt  and  pepper  shakers.
