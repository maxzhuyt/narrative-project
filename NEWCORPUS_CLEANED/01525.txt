One  impossible  day  of  an  impossible  month  of  an  im-  possible  year.  Noboru  Watanabe,  where  have  you  gone?  I  think.  Did  the  windup  bird  stop  winding  your  spring?  It's  a  regular  poem:  Noboru  Watanabe  Where  have  you  gone?  Did  the  windup  bird  Stop  winding  your  spring?  I'm  halfway  through  my  beer  when  the  telephone  begins  to  ring.  &quot;Get  that,  will  you?&quot;  I  shout  into  the  living-room  darkness.  &quot;No  way!  You  get  it  yourself,&quot;  says  my  wife.  &quot;I  don't  want  to  get  it,&quot;  I  say.  No  one  answers,  and  it  keeps  on  ringing.  The  ringing  stirs  up  the  loose  dust  floating  in  the  dark.  Neither  my  wife  nor  I  ventures  one  word.  Me  drinking  my  beer,  her  sobbing  away.  Twenty-four  rings  before  I  lose  count  and  just  let  the  thing  ring.  You  can't  keep  counting  forever. "
