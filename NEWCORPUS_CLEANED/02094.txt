Instead  of  saying  this,  I  said,  &quot;How  do  you  expect  me  to  sup-  port  ten  children?&quot;  The  colonel's  wife  didn't  like  me  much,  but  she  had  said  that  after  the  war  there  were  a  few  people  she  could  introduce  me  to.  She  had  mentioned  something  about  radio  broadcasting,  and  I  liked  the  idea.  Ju-  liette  was  still  kneeling,  with  only  part  of  the  hideous  dress  buttoned  up.  I  looked  down  at  her  bent  head.  She  must  have  beenthinking  that  she  had  tied  herself  to  a  man  with  no  money,  no  prospects,  and  no  connections.  Who  wasn't  entirely  single.  Who  might  be  put  on  a  charge  for  making  a     false  declaration.  Who  had  a  broken  nose  and  a  permanent  limp.  Who,  so  far,  had  never  finished  anything  he'd  started.
