Jean  opened  the  heavy  door  and  looked  out.  Through  a  screen  door  shimmering  with  captured  water  she  saw  that  the  sun  had  appeared,  as  on  the  first  day  of  creation.  A  few  silver  raindrops  still  flashed  across  the  radiance.  It  had  finished  raining,  like  this,  on  one  of  her  mother's  good  days,  just  before  she  had  to  be  sent  away  to  McCoy.  Jean  remembered  the  after-  noon  as  ifit  were  something  she  had  all  along  been  trying  to  remember.  She  had  taken  her  mother  to  buy  plants  at  a  nursery  outside  Rochester;  the  rain  stopped  as  they  were  getting  out  of  the  car.
