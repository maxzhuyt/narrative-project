He  had  no  wish  to  live  with  his  father,  but  his  father  came  to  the  school  to  see  him  sometimes,  and  he  had  always  had  the  feeling  that  if  he  ever  got  fed  up  with  living  at  home  with  his  mother  and  Martha,  his  father  would  always  have  him.  Nobody  had  told  him  that  by  becoming  a  Catholic  he  had  made  it  impossible  for  his  father  to  have  him.
