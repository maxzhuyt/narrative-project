

The New Yorker, December 25, 2006 P. 70
Short story about a young cemetery worker's affair with an older woman doctor in the town of Pluto, North Dakota… I used to have a girlfriend who was slightly bigger than me. In bed, C, moved with the agility of a high-school wrestler. There was always a little squeeze bear full of honey, which she drank from the bottle… I was seventeen when I began digging graves for the Pluto dead. My job at the summer was supposed to last a summer, to fill the time until I went to college. But once I started having sex with C. I couldn't leave sex or her or Pluto. C. owned the only house that had a yard abutting the cemetery. I could walk out of my equipment shed and enter C.'s back door without being seen… Narrator lives with his ailing father, a retired lawyer. One day C. tells him she is marrying a general contractor named Ted. C.'s marriage so aggrieved me that I started taking correspondence courses in my father's and grandfather's profession. For a year after C.'s wedding, I tried not even to look in the direction of her house. One dusty evening, I walked between the graves and through the back yard like I used to and there she was, waiting for me on the back steps. She'd sat there after work every night all year… Ted built many of the newer houses in Pluto. He was also responsible for most of the least attractive buildings in town. I'd hated him even before he married the woman I loved. Once we got back together, I had to avoid Ted… Narrator returns home after a night with C. to find his father crumpled on the floor of the basement. It was time to sell the house and put him in a safe place where he could talk to other old people… No one will buy the house, except for Ted, who wants to knock it down. I finished my law degree as I waited for the house to sell. One night he falls asleep while studying for the bar exam. All of a sudden I woke up in blackness, alive to desolate knowledge. I had wasted my life on a woman. I called the real estate agent… The narrator and C. confront Ted as he and his crew are destroying the house. Ted accidentally disturbs a beehive and is attacked by the bees… The narrator moves away and becomes a lawyer. He returns to Pluto and sees that C. has not aged well. He visits the plot of his old house and lies down on the grass where the living room couch had been.
