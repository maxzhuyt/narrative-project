He  thanked  Alpha  for  her  patience  and  hospitality,  once  again  apologized  for  his  rush,  reminded  her  to  give  Martin  his  greetings,  and  then  strode  to  the  front  door.  Jerome  and  Charles  and  Tim  stood  at  the  end  ofthe  hall  and  watched  while  he  said  goodbye  to  their  mother  and,  as  she  inclined  her  head,  quickly  gave  her  his  blessing. Their  mother  closed  the  door.  She  stood  with  her  forehead  against  it  and  her  hand  on  the  knob,  as  though  ex-  amining  her  thoughts  with  such  inten-  sity  she  couldn't  move,  and  then  turned  to  them. But  they  knew  what  she  was  feeling;  now  the  Russells,  as  well  as  the  Halvorsons,  would  always  occupy  a  part  of  their  house.
