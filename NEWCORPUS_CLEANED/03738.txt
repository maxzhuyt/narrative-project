THE  Army  duck  advanced  steadily  through  mud  and  water,  the  soldier  at  the  wheel  frowning  with  con-  centration  as  he  steered  his  course,  his  younger  companion  gazing  about  with  a  tourist's  interest  at  the  weird  effects  of  the  flood.  It  was  early  morning.  They  had  started  out  on  the  road  from  their  barracks  to  the  town  at  midnight  and,  after  two  hours'  sleep  in  the  town,  had  been  called  from  their  beds  to  begin  rescue  work.
