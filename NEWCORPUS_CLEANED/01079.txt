She  directs  a  retire-  ment  community  in  [REDACTED],  and  today,  full  of  hope,  she  leads  several  of  her  charges  on  a  walking  tour  of  a  desert  park  just  outside  the  city.  They  have  ar-  rived  by  van-Karen,  five  residents  of  Homewood  Estates,  and  a  driver  named  Darrell  Jettie.  Single  file,  they  plod  down  a  marked  cinder  trail,  past  wild-  flowers  and  dwarf  pions  and  twisted  old  saguaros.  The  day  is  a  scorcher,  but  the  group  moves  along  dutifully,  the  men  chatting  about  golf,  the  women  dabbing  at  their  foreheads  as  Karen  reads  aloud  from  a  park  brochure. For  six  weeks,  since  hiring  Darrell  as  a  part-time  driver,  Karen  has  entertained  robust,  minutely  detailed  romantic  fan-  tasies.  She  cannot  help  herself.
