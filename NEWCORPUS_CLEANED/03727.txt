Mrs.  Barrett  stood  up  and  switched  the  light  on  again.  &quot;Yes,  Father,&quot;  she  said,  &quot;you  got  your  wish.&quot;  Squinting  against  the  sudden  glare  of  the  light,  the  old  priest's  eyes  once  more  sought  out  his  cake.  Streams  of  smoke  were  rising  from  the  twelve  black  wicks,  filling  the  air  with  a  sad  fragrance.  &quot;'Tell  us  what  it  was,  Father,&quot;  said  Mr.  Barrett.  &quot;What  did  youwish?&quot;  The  old  priest  raised  his  eyes  again.  His  face  had  an  air  of  absent-minded  abstraction,  and  his  wish,  when  he  re-  vealed  it,  was  no  wish  at  all  but  the  simple  admission  that  he  hadgone  past  all  wishing.  &quot;For  the  grace  of  a  happy  birthday,&quot;  he  said.
