At  the  end  of  the  summer,  I  hap-  pened  to  be  passing  through  my  grand-  mother's  parlor  one  afternoon,  quick-  ly,  to  pick  up  something  I  had  left  there  before  I  should  have  to  stop  and  talk  to  any  of  the  visitors-or,  rather,  be  talked  at-when  I  heard  a  name  that  made  me  scurry  on  through  and  then  hide  behind  the  door  to  listen  fur-  ther.  I  peeked  inside.  Someone  in  a  flower-sprigged  sheer  with  ruffles  was  trying  to  read  something  but  also  trying  not  to  laugh.  Only  my  grandmother  was  not  smiling.  I  believe  she  knew  I  was  there  beyond  the  doorway,  listen-  ing.
