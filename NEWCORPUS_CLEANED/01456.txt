The  weather  had  closed  in  and  snow  was  starting  to  fall.  &quot;I'll  call  the  doctor  about  the  house  in  acouple  of  days,&quot;  Ralph  said.  &quot;I  don't  want  to  push  too  hard  too  fast.&quot;  &quot;Whatever,&quot;  Heagan  said.  He  hoisted  himself  into  his  pickup  with  a  construction-worker's  move,  hauling  with  both  hands  from  above  and  swing,  ing  his  feet  into  the  cab.  He  stuck  his  head  out  the  window  as  he  backed  down  the  drive.  Ralph  watched  the  pickup  go  up  the  road.  It  dimmed  and  then  disappeared  in  the  descending  gray  flakes.  He  found  himself  wondering  what  was  so  bad  about  calling  themselves  a  team.  As  soon  as  the  company  fell  apart,  every-  body  started  getting  into  trouble.  At  least  when  Bilt-Rite  was  on  a  job,  things  never  got  completely  out  of  hand.  But  Heagan  thought  all  this  was  mushy,  and  so  would  Rollo  if  Ralph  were  ever  foolish  enough  to  ask  him.  It  bothered  Ralph  that  he  was  less  tough  than  his  father.  Rollo  didn't  even  think  that  what  Ralph  was  going  through  right  now  was  a  very  hard  time.
