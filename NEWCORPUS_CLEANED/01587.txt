POTATO  HEAD  HATS,&quot;  BULBS,&quot;  &quot;PLASTIC  SUSHI  REPLICAS,&quot;  &quot;FAN  BELTS,&quot;  &quot;LITTLE  RED  MONKEYS.&quot;  He  showed  me  the  plans  for  a  new  game  called  Car  Crash,  involving  bottle  caps,  miniature  Christmas  light  bulbs,  tin-whistle  si-  rens,  and  cans  of  some  knockoff  red  Play-Doh  from  [REDACTED],  and  then,  crouching  down  on  the  floor  and  reaching  in  behind  the  carton  of  gum  arabic,  drew  out  a  large  square  box.  &quot;This  is  going  to  be  my  next  toy,&quot;  he  said.  &quot;I'm  calling  it  Treasure.&quot;  This  time  the  word  &quot;Treasure&quot;  was  machine-stencilled  on  the  box's  sides,  in  large  letters,  along  with  the  name  of  a  leading  British  toy  manufacturer  and  the  two  words  &quot;Spanish  Main,&quot;  in  Old  English  type.  &quot;'They  tried  to  market  it  over  here,  but  it  stiffed  ear-  ly,&quot;  he  explained,  opening  the  seal  on  the  box  with  his  thumbnail.  &quot;Levinsky  made  a  killing  in  [REDACTED]  on  amisdelivered  shipment  of  game  pieces.&quot;  I  watched  his  face  for  any  sign  that  he  was  toying  with  me,  but  there  was  none;  he  seemed  to  want  only  to  show  me,  with  a  hint  of  desperation,  what  was  inside  the  crate,  as  though  the  hardest  part  of  it  for  him  had  been  having  no  one  in  whom  to  confide  the  secrets  of  his  fabulous  vault.  He  lifted  the  flaps  to  reveal  a  king's  ransom,  a  cool  million,  in  cardboard  doubloons,  painted  gold  and  dimly  glittering  in  the  basement  light,  and  I  won-  dered  if  this  was  what  had  been  in  the  box  I'd  buried  in  Mrs.  Colodny's  yard,  or  ifit  had  been  some  other  trea-  sure  entirely.  I  knew  little  about  the  subject,  but  I  hoped  that  once  you  had  buried  a  treasure  you  did  not  have  to  keep  reburying  it  again  and  again.  &quot;It's  supposedly  real  gold  dust  in  the  paint,&quot;  Harry  said.  &quot;That  was  the  gimmick,  I  guess.&quot;  He  handed  me  a  thick  coin,  and  I  examined  it.  It  bore  an  illegible  mock  inscription  and  a  crude  cartoon  of  an  emperor's  head,  and  as  I  fingered  it  some  of  its  lustre  came  away  on  my  hands.  Harry  was  looking  right  at  me  now,  with  a  fevered  smile,  and  once  more  I  didn't  know  what  to  say,  but  there  was  no  one  else  there,  and  I  had  to  say  something.  &quot;We're  rich,&quot;  I  said.
