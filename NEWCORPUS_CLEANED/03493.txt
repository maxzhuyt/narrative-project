One  early  morning  in  Elul,  when  the  rabbi  was  sitting  in  his  study  writing  commentary  and  drinking  tea  from  a  samovar,  Tevye  the  Night  Watchman  knocked  at  his  door. Half  the  town  followed  the  body  to  the  cemetery.  The  brothers  were  notified,  and  came  later  to  sell  the  house  and  order  a  stone  for  their  sister's  grave.  It  was  clear  to  everyone  that  the  man  who  had  appeared  with  Liebe  Yentl  on  the  road  to  Radom  was  the  dead  fid-  dler  of  Pinchev.  Dunya,  Zise  Feige's  former  servant,  told  the  women  that  Liebe  Yentl  had  not  been  able  to  forget  her  dead  bridegroom  Ozer,  and  that  Ozer  had  become  a  Dybbuk  in  order  to  prevent  the  marriage  to  Shmelke  Motl.
