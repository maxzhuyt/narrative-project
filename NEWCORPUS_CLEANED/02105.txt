THE  OIL-IN-LANGUAGE  ATTEMPT  A  SraremeEnt  or  Purrosc  The  Oil-in-Language  Attempt  is  a  consortium  of  words  and  gifts  which  seeks  to  select  word-elements  and  gift-  worthiness  aspects  which  will  &quot;syn-  ergize&quot;  in  tandem.  The  tandem  ele-  ments,  including  permissions  from  twelve  oil  companies  and  word-  phrase-sentence  projects  from  muse-  ums  and  universities,  are  mounted  in  travelling  units  that  move  throughout  the  country  without  footnotes,  in  most  instances,  seeking  a  &quot;new  audience&quot;  outside  traditional  funding  constraints.  The  participation  of  universities  and  museums  is  perceived  as  vital  as  a  way  of  urging  new  approaches  to  funding  constraints.  The  &quot;premise&quot;  of  this  project,  from  the  point  of  view  of  the  Oil  Permission  Fund  (the  Funder  of  Ultimate  Responsibility),  is  that  by  purchasing  words  outright  footnoting  may  be  reduced  and  comprehensibility  enhanced.  The  Attempt  does  not  col-  lect  copyright  fees  on  the  word-units  it     has  funded  and  in  many  instances  has  agreed  to  forgo  credit-taking  on  a  word-phrase-sentence  basis  to  reduce  distraction.
