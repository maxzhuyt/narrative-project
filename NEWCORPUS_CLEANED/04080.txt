Then  either  I  awake  in  despair  or  am  waked  by  the  sound  of  rain  on  the  palms.  I  think  of  some  farmer  who,  hearing  the  noise  of  rain,  will  stretch  his  lame  bones  and  smile,  feeling  that  the  rain  is  falling  into  his  lettuce  and  his  cabbages,  his  hay  and  his  oats,  his  parsnips  and  his  corn.  I  think  of  some  plumber  who,  waked  by  the  rain,  will  smile  at  a  vision  of  the  world  in  which  all  the  drains  are  miraculously  cleansed  and  free.  Right-  angle  drains,  crooked  drains,  root-  choked  and  rusty  drains  all  gurgle  and  discharge  their  waters  into  the  sea.
