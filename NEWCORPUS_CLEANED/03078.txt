Then  I  fetched  her  boots  and  put  them  by  her  bed,  in  the  corner  of  the  room  under  the  only  window.  It  was  the  meanest  habitation  I  have  ever  seen.  There  was  nothing,  except  a  fewdog  roses  in  a  jam  jar,  to  soften  its  comfortlessness;  nothing  that  was  not  useful.  &quot;Aha,  but  she  was  the  frisky  bit,  all  right,&quot;  she  mumbled,  &quot;and  she  tied  her  hair  with  ivy.  All  the  way  from  Tuam  to  [REDACTED],  and  the  frost  like  nails  to  the  feet.  And  the  thieves  and  rogues,  they  wouldn't  pay  the  half  the  pig  was  worth.  And  she'd  have  trudged  the  twelve  miles  home  in  the  boots  that  was  hurting,  but  for  the  Christian  man.  Frisky,  aha,  and  come  all  the  way  like  the  lady.&quot;  I  filled  her  mug  almost  to  the  brim  and  topped  it  with  a  little  of  the  whiskey,  and  when  I'd  curled  her  fingers  round  the  mug  handle  I  put  another  turf  on  the  fire  and  left  her.  She  was  singing  again.  Outside  her  door,  the  chickens  had  come  to  be  fed,  and  these,  apart  from  a  pair  of  hoodic  crows,  were  the  last  living  creatures  I  saw  until  I  got  back  onto  the  road  to  Mallow.
