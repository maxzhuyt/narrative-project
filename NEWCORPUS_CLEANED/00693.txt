Almost  all  the  commuters  stood  on  the  other  side,  with  Vadim.  They  were  going  up  the  line,  into  the  city,  to  work  at  real  jobs.  I  was  going  down  the  line,  to  sell  the  Cinderella  dream  and  a  laxative  hyped  as  a  magic  potion.  More  than  once,  I  thought  I  saw  something  like  pity  in  Vadim's  expression.  Every  Monday,  I  fantasized  about  quitting.  I  imagined  myself  tearing  down  the  tea  poster,  like  Evelina,  then  march-  ing  into  Solomon's  office  and  telling  him  that  he  was  a  liar  and  a  crook,  that  he  dis-  gusted  me.  But,  as  the  week  progressed,  I'd  remember  those  horrible  days  Td  spent  with  the  Help  Wanted  section,  and  my  resolve  would  fade.  One  Tuesday  in  early  March,  Adella  called  me  at  home  and  asked  me  to  come  to  the  office,  even  though  I  didn't  have  a  class  that  day.  &quot;Go,  go-he's  waiting  for  you,&quot;  she  said,  pointing  to  Solomon's  room  when  I  arrived.  Solomon  was  standing  by  his  desk,  staring  down,  his  fingers  rubbing  his  temples.  &quot;Close  the  door!&quot;  he  com-  manded  in  a  grating  voice.  I  closed  the  door.  &quot;I  saw  one  of  your  students  today,&quot;  he  said,  without  looking  up.  &quot;She  told  me  that  you  have  been  teaching  her  at  her  home.  Behind  my  back!&quot;  He  made  a  sharp  movement  and  knocked  over  one  of  the  penises.  I  instinctively  picked  it  up.  &quot;Youcame  here  with  your  fake  di-  ploma,but  I  trusted  you.  I  gave  you  a  chance,  didn't  I?  I  took  you  on  and  I  paid  for  that  bullshit  article  about  you.  And  still  you  felt  that  you  could  steal  from  me?  Just  like  that?&quot;  His  left  eye  was  twitching.  He  looked  old  and  genuinely  hurt.  He  slumped  into  his  chair  and  shook  his  head.  It  was  then  that  Ireal-  ized  I  was  still  holding  a  sticky,  gummy  penis  in  my  hand.  I  put  it  back  on  the  desk  and  walked  to  the  door.  &quot;You're  a  liar  and  a  thief!  You  disgust  me!&quot;he  yelled  at  my  back.  When  I  came  out,  Adella  reached  under  her  desk  and  pushed  a  box  full  of  my  videos  toward  me  without  a  word.  It  was  raining,  and  the  bottom  of  the  box  gave  out  halfway  to  the  subway.  I  gathered  up  the  videos  and  carried  them  in  a  messy  pile  in  my  arms.  &quot;Pretty  Woman.&quot;  &quot;Working  Girl.&quot;  &quot;Once  Upon  a  Time  in  America.&quot;  The  videos  on  top  of  the  stack  kept  sliding  off,  and  I  had  to  crouch  and  retrieve  them  with  one  shaking  hand  while  cradling  the  rest  with  my  other  arm.  For  some  reason,  it  was  this  activity-crouching,  cradling,  and  picking  thevideos  up  off  the  wet  asphalt-that  really  made  me  feel  like  a  Tiar  and  a  thief.  I  was  almost  at  the  sub-  way  when  it  occurred  to  me  that  I  didn't  have  to  carry  them  at  all.  I  put  the  vid-  eos  down,  in  three  neat  stacks  by  a  pile  of  discarded  furniture  on  the  sidewalk,  and  walked  away.
