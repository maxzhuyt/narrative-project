The  bar  is  rosewood  and  a  hundred  feet  or  more  in  length;  the  back  bar,  rosewood  also  and  elaborately  carved,  is  dominated  by  a  single,  greening,  ninety-foot  plate-  glass  mirror,  which  reflects  the  backs  of  several  hundred  bottles  with  old  and  unfamiliar  labels  (e.g.,  S.S. My  mind  moves  upward  a  level,  out  of  sleep  and  into  semi-consciousness.  Isee  early-morning  light-a  lighter  blue  behind  the  deep-  blue  curtains  of  my  bedroom.  In  the  same  moment,  half  awake,  I  see  also  that  the  dream,  not  frightful  enough  to  be  a  nightmare,  will  not  propel  me  to  the  surface  of  full  wakefulness,  and  thatI  now  have  a  chance  to  choose  its  ending.  My  mind  works  on  the  material  it  is  still  taking  part  in  (in  one  of  its  cham-  bers,  I  am  still  clinging  to  the  shaft  of  the  stairway).  I  can  be  simply  ab-  solved  by  my  friend's  testimony.  I  can  be  as  easily  condemned  by  his  failure  to  recognize  me,  at  one  extreme,  or  to  corroborate  my  evidence,  at  the  other.  Or  all  four  of  us-myself,  my  friend,  the  two  strangers-can  be  made  equal  victims  of  an  inadvertent  error.  I  have  my  ending.
