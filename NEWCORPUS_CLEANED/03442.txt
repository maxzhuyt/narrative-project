Rice  can  be  cooked  in  two  basic  ways-right  and  wrong.  Right,  for  me,  results  in  separate  and  unsoggy  grains,  tender  but  still  with  a  little  elasticity  in  them.  I  know  this  can  be  attained  by  cooking  it  in  a  great  deal  of  quickly  boiling  water  and  washing  it  before  and  after,  thus  robbing  it  of  almost  everything  but  its  innate  nature;  that  it  can  be  done  by  using  a  West-  ernized  version  of  the  age-old  Oriental  method,  to  be  found  in  any  reputable  kitchen  manual;  and  that  it  can  even  be  done,  in  a  pinch,  with  a  packaged  five-minute  facsimile.
