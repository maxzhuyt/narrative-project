The  tears  hurt  him  and  covered  him  with  a  pain  that  seemed  to  grow  more  unbearable  everyminute,  but  what  hurt  most  of  all  was  his  inability  to  tell  his  sister  that  he  was  not  crying  for  Rose,  because  he  really  and  truly  felt  no  grief  for  Rose,  but  that  he  was  crying  for  the  lack  of  grief,  because  surely  poor  Rosehad  de-  served  more  than  a  casual  dismissal  from  life,  and  that  most  of  all  he  was  crying  simply  and  solely  because  he  was  sad.  He  was  sad  and  he  was  crying,  and  that  is  all  there  was  to  it.  But  he  could  not  speak  to  tell  his  sister  that,  and  she  con-  tinued  to  watch  him,  helpless  with  tears  herself  and  murmuring  about  how  hap-  py  Rose  was  in  Heaven,  and  he  could  not  speak  to  her  to  tell  her  that  it  was  all  only  a  masquerade  and  that  he  was  only  a  sham  of  a  man,  and  after  a  long  time,  when  he  finally  got  command  of  him-  self,  it  no  longer  seemed  worthwhile  to  tell  her,  and  the  way  it  worked  out  he  never  told  her,  and  never  told  any-  body.
