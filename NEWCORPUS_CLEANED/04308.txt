They  wanted  a  chance  to  get  in  there.  &quot;Oh,  no!&quot;  she  cried.  &quot;I've  only  come  to  fetch  home  my  father.&quot;  They  nodded  their  heads  sagaciously.  When  she  went  to  pass  through  the:  gateway,  though,  they  still  blocked  her  way.  And  when  they  asked  her  another  question,  she  couldn't  tell  at  the  time  if  it  was  asked  slyly  or  innocently.  &quot;Who  does  he  be  talking  to  in  there,  Miss?&quot;  they  asked.  Alice  hesitated.  &quot;Himself,  I  sup-  pose,&quot;  she  said,  trving  to  be  frank  with  them,  but  she  saw  they  received  her  ex-  planation  dubiously.  &quot;You  should  be  at  home  at  this  hour!&quot;  she  said.  &quot;Your  mothers  will  be  looking  for  you!&quot;  At  that  moment,  further  down  the  road,  a  voice  called  &quot;Chil-dren'!  Chil-  dren!&quot;  It  carried  well,  that  voice,  like  a  coin  tossed  into  the  air-an  unrelated  thing,  inconsequent,  like  the  cuckoo's  call.  Alice  listened  for  a  minute,  and  then  went  into  the  brightness  of  the  ceme-  tery.  Because  roots  are  troublesome  when  a  grave  is  being  dug,  there  were  no  trees  in  the  cemetery,  and  even  with-  out  the  light  of  the  moon  there  was  a  whiteness  everywhere,  of  marble  slabs  and  ornaments.  &quot;Father,  you  must  come  home,&quot;  she     called  to  him.  &quot;You're  carrying  this  too  far.  You  can't  make  her  live  for-  ever,  no  matter  what  you  do!&quot;  She  was  harsh  and  bitter.  Yet  when  he  answered  her,  he  was  so  gentle  and  conciliatory.  &quot;Forever  is  a  long  time  indeed,&quot;  he  said  sedately.  &quot;But  I  know  she'd  like  to  be  remembered  as  long  as  possible! Trees  by  the  gate  threw  one  great  mass  of  shadow  over  half  the  graveyard,  but  at  the  edge  the  shadow  was  serrated;  each  young  leaf  cut  out  its  own  sharp  shape.  And  she  could  count  every  peb-  ble  of  the  gravel.  Here  and  therea  pebble,  larger  than  the  others,  cast  a  pebble  of  shadow,  and  the  path  was  dap-  pled.  As  she  stood  there,  the  woman's  voice  called  out  again,  the  woman  away  in  the  dark  and  Alice  in  the  light.  &quot;Chil-  dren-chil-dren'!&quot;  Alice  waited.  The  voice  fell  to  earth.  After  a  minute  it  came  again,  but  near-  er,  and  now  it  was  a  purely  human  voice.  &quot;Children?  Where  are  you?&quot;  Was  there  a  giggle  in  the  dark  high  grass  inside  the  gate?  &quot;I  hear  you!  I  know  you're  there!  Come  along  home  at  once. Chil-  dren!&quot;  The  giggling  this  time  was  ill-con-  cealed,  and,  somewhere  near,  a  fat  bot-  tom  flopped  backward  into  the  tall  grass.  &quot;Oh,  you  devils!&quot;  cried  the  woman.  &quot;Stay  out  all  night  if  you  like!
