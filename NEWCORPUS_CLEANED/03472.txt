It  was  quiet.  Not  many  other  children  played  in  the  yard.  There  were  only  a  few  cars  moving  on  the  street.  His  father's  small  truck  sat  in  the  yard.  Philip  climbed  into  the  back.  It  was  black  and  shiny,  and  had  leather  flaps  rolled  and  tied  in  under  the  roof.  Often  he  heard  a  hollow  voice  booming  from  some-  where  in  his  big  brick  house,  &quot;Hey,  kid,  get  the  hell  out  of  that  truck!&quot;  Philip  shouted,  &quot;Who  said  that?&quot;  He  put  his  foot  upon  the  tail  and  frowned.  &quot;This  is  my  fether's  truck!&quot;  he  said.  There  was  never  an  answer.  "
