You  cut  a  length  of  buckram  into  a  body  shape  andsew  your  lady  into  it  over  the  minimum  of  under-  wear.  I  did  this  with  my  grandmother,  basting  the  buckram  on  her  body  with  only  an  exact  inch  to  spare.  She  thought  she  would  never  get  out  of  it  again. When  I  had  perfected  the  sewing  on  the  buck-  ram  with  even,  small  backstitching,  I  filled  the  dummy  with  fine-teased  raw  wool. Needleworkers,  on  the  other  hand,  never  go  out  of  date,  but  I  was  always  a  needlewoman  of  a  special  sort.  There  is  a  big  difference  between  the  seams  that  are  right  for  velvet  and  those  for  chiffon,  and  I  have  devised  ways  of  sewing  a  lace  dress  where  you  wouldn't  know  there  was  any  seam  at  all.
