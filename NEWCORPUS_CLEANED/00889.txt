In  Corry's  workshop  she  remained  longer  than  she  usually  did  on  her  morn-  ing  visit  to  the  saints  who  had  become  her  friends:  St.  Lawrence  with  his  grid-  iron,  St.  Gabriel  the  messenger,  St.  Clare  of  Assisi,  St.  Thomas  the  Apostle  and  blind  St.  Lucy,  St.  Catherine,  St.  [REDACTED].  Corry  had  made  them  live  for  her  and  she  felt  the  first  faint  slipping  away  of  her  anger  as  they  returned  her  gaze  with  undisturbed  tranquillity.  Touched  by  it,  lost  in  its  peace,  shesensed  their  resigna-  tion,  too.  he  world,  not  she,  had  failed.
