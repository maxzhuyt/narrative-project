The  first  day  beyond  the  days  her  mother  had  lived  seemed  a  terrible,  weighty  darkness  to  her,  a  final  lid  coming  down.  How  could  she  live  that  day?  She  remembered  her-  that  woman  who  wept  away  the  after-  noons,  grief  in  her  eyes  even  when  no  tears  fell.  If  she,  too,  died  in  the  mid-  dle  of  her  life,  if  she  died  this  minute,  throwing  herself  under  the  wheels  of  the  bus  that  now  inched  its  way  toward  them,  she  would  die  with  nothing,  no  skill  at  living,  no  information.
