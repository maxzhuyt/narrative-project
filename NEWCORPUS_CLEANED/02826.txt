I  had  intended  to  nap,  but  the  boys  will  be  home  in  just  a  few  minutes.  I  hang  up  my  long  dress,  go  back  into  the  kitch-  en,  and  wash  the  lunch  dishes.  The  boys  will  want  me  to  play  with  them,  read  with  them,  make  them  snacks,  do  some  of  the  things  that  mothers  do  for  their  children,  that  my  mother  did  for  me.  I  sit  at  the  table,  but  the  kitchen  is  too  still,  too  quiet.  My  head  is  heavy  and  I  feel  very  tired.  The  buzzer  rings:  the  boys  are  home.  I  wait  by  the  door,  sniffing.  They  fling  it  open,  and  their  eyes  look  round  with  surprise.  I  explain,  &quot;After  you  left  I  decided  to  stay  home.  I  have  a  little  cold.  .  ..&quot;  They  jump  up  and  down,  shrieking,  &quot;Mommy's  home,  Mommy's  home!&quot;  They  are  so  happy  they  punch  each  other.  They  are  so  happy  they  are  punching  me. "
