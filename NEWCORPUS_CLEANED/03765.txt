The  two  men  chanted,  sitting  cross-  legged,  with  their  audience  encircling  them,  in  the  heat  of  the  party  cellar,  until  one  person  after  another  became  drowsy  and  either  curled  to  sleep  on  the  spot  or  stretched  and  departed  into  thesharp,  dark  air  of  early  morning.  The  cellar  was  aired  merelyby  vents  in  the  upper  walls,  and  by  cracks  in  the  ceiling,  which  let  in  some  small  whis-  pers  of  the  fresh  night  from  the  open  windows  above.
