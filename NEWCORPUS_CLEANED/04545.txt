He  lighted  it,  then  drew  his  knees  up  and  clasped  his  arms  around  them  as  he  smoked.  He  could  hear  Lau-  ra's  deep,  steady  breathing,  and  from  far  away,  out  the  window,  he  thought  he  heard  the  cry  of  the  owl.  He  listened  closely,  but  he  didn't  hear  it  again.  Then  he  reasoned  that  it  didn't  make  much  difference  whether  he  heard  the  owl  or  not,  because  he  knew  that  he  wasn't  going  to  sleep  that  night  anyway. "
