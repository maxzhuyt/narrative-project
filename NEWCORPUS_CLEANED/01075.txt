The  seismic  moment  of  an  earth-  quake  is  measured  by  using  the  logarithm  of  moment-known  as  the  magnitude-  rather  than  the  moment  itself.  Thus  all  earthquakes,  small  to  large,  are  typically  ranked  from  one  to  nine,  each  unit  of  magnitude  representing  a  tenfold  increase  in  strength.  A  quake  of  the  ninth  magni-  tude  is  a  billion  times  more  powerful  than  a  first-  magnitude  shock.  This  system  of  measurement  is  named  after  the  Ameri-  can  seismologist  Charles  Richter.
