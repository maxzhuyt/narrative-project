I  can  picture  them  still  on  the  edge  of  a  Wilcombe  lawn  party,  with  their  pale  brushed  blond  heads  like  candles  burning  in  the  summer  sunlight,  a  ribbon  or  a  plastic  barrette  attached  for  the  occasion. We  were  in  our  late  twen-  ties  then,  young  at  being  old,  the  best  of  times.  The  summer  smells  of  bug  spray  on  the  lawn  and  fresh  mint  in  the  gin,  the  young  wives  so  healthy  in  their  brown  skins  and  sundresses,  the  children  still  small  and  making  a  flockin  the  uncut  grass  beyond  the  lawn,  running  and  tumbling,  the  pastel  dresses  getting  stained  with  green,  their  noise  com-  ing  and  going  in  the  field  as  a  higher-  pitched  echo  of  ours,  creating  their  own  world  underfoot  as  the  liquor  and  the  sunlight  soaked  in.
