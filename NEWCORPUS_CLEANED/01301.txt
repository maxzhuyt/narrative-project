Now  the  marshal,  back  there  in  the  dark  with  his  shot-  gun,  was  saying,  &quot;Fellas,  this  is  Dale  Crowe  Junior,  another  one  believes  it's  the  system's  fault  he's  ill-tempered  and  feels  it's  O.K.  to  assault  people.&quot;  Saying  then,  after  a  minute,  &quot;I  know  a  fella  sixty-seven  years  old,  got  rich  off  our  economic  system  running  a  sports  book,  has  more  money'n  he  can  ever  spend.  But  this  man,  with  all  his  advan-  tages,  doesn't  know  what  to  do  with  himself.  Mopes  around,  drinks  too  much,  gets  everybody  upset  and  worried  so  they'll  feel  sorry  for  him.&quot;  The  car-jacker  next  to  Dale  Junior  said,  &quot;You  was  to  lemme  go,  I'll  see  the  man  don't  bother  you  no  more.&quot;  Dale  Junior  thought  the  marshal  would  tell  him  to  keep  his  mouth  shut,  maybe  poke  him  with  the  shotgun.  But  nothing  happened  like  that,  and  there  was  a  silence,  no  sound  from  back  there  in  the  dark  until  the  marshal  said,  &quot;You  miss  the  point.  This  friend  of  mine  isn't  bothering  me  any,  he's  his  own  problem.  Same  as  you  fellas.  I  don't  take  what  you  did  personally.  You  understand?  Want  to  lean  on  you.
