Once  they  reached  the  beach  the  sand  was  cooler,  but  the  roar  of  the  water  was  fiercer  than  up  on  the  dune.  Jim  could  taste  the  salty  water,  broken  up  and  falling  through  the  air.  He  reached  up  and  grabbed  Uncle  Al's  hand.  &quot;We  probably  shouldn't  get  in  that  water,  Jim,&quot;  Uncle  Al  said.  &quot;We  don't  know  anything  about  that  water.&quot;  &quot;I'm  not  going  to,&quot;  Jim  said. They  looked  for  a  while  at  the  place  in  the  distance  where  the  blue  sky  and  the  blue  water  became  the  same  thing.  At  the  edge  of  the  surf,  a  small  white  bird  with  long  legs  ran  up  and  down.
