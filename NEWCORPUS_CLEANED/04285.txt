'The  distressing  business  was  the  more  out  of  key  because  Clawdd,  which  lies  in  one  of  the  most  secluded  valleys  of  [REDACTED],  is  noted  for  its  small  but  old-established  theological  college.  Otherwise,  the  place  has  little  to  recom-  mend  it  except  the  several  pure  water-  falls  and  a  breed  of  sheep  that,  nurtured  on  moistly  succulent  pastures,  becomes  the  tenderest  mutton  in  the  land.  Jane,  unencumbered  by  a  bathing  suit,  took  her  swim  in  apool  formed  by  the  river  in  a  glade  just  below  her  home,  outside  the  village.  Fishing  rights  in  the  stretch  of  river  tumbling  thereabouts  belonged  to  her  bad-tempered  mother,  who  rented  them  by  the  season  to  rich  anglers  coming  from  elsewhere.  One  critic  suggested,  that  same  Saturday  eve-  ning,  in  the  Hawk  Inn,  that  she  had  been  set  bathing  in  the  pool  by  her  mother  as  a  trap-a  criticism  based  on  the  fact  that  the  Morgans  couldn't  al-  ways  lay  their  hands  on  ready  money  to  pay  their  bills.  But  there  was  dissent.  Why  shouldn't  she  enjoy  lolling  about  in  out-of-doors  water?  Jane  was  twenty,  and  although  in  school,  and  later,  she  had  been  ferocious  at  all  en-  ergetic  sports,  indolence  had  begun  to  undo  her  that  summer.  After  climbing  out  of  the  pool,  she  took  a  long  timeover  drying  herself,  standing  on  the  daisy-sprinkled  bank.  Heavy-jowled  for  her  age,  and  with  small  gray  eyes  set  too  deeply  in  her  head,  she  owned,  nevertheless,  a  well-off  body  such  as  is  seen,  as  a  rule,  only  on  a  plinth  in  a  museum  or,  alive  and  in  a  bathing  suit,  standing  on  the  winner's  podium  at  a  seaside  fete  while  the  brass  band  waits  to  strike  up. Her  amiable  spaniel,  a  bird  or  two,  and  a  few  droning  bees  seemed  to  be  her  sole  companions  in  enjoyment  of  the  purring  sunshine.  She  was  on  her  mother's  enclosed  land,  and  no  anglers  had  arrived  that  afternoon.
