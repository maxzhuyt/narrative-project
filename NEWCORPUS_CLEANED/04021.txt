Even  on  cold  days,  when  the  [REDACTED]  Ricans  down  the  street,  wrapped  up  to  their  noses,  rush  to  get  indoors,  the  lady  is  out  there,  pale  but  brilliant  between  her  shining  wings. He  bought  his  stamps,  I  paid  for  my  cigarettes,  and  we  walked  out  together.  Both  of  us,  I  think,  felt  the  same  pleasure  in  meeting  each  other,  in  talking.  There  is  a  strain,  slight  but  perpetual,  on  those  upper  West  Side  streets,  a  strain  that  grows,  partly,  I  suppose,  because  not  even  the  usual  small  talk-about  the  weather,  about  the  news-can  be  easily  exchanged,  partly  because  noise  in  a  foreign  lan-  guage  always  sounds  louder  than  it  does  in  your  own,  and  partly  because  pov-  erty  next  door,  or  near,  is  frightening.  Conversation  seemed  to  relieve  the  strain  in  both  of  us.  We  were  talking  as  if  our  meeting  and  our  walking  to-  gether  were  normal,  natural.
