The  Hilltoppers'  style  wasn't  exactly  Big  Bill  Broonzy  and  it  wasn't  rock  and  roll  yet  either-it  was  sort  of  like  what  would  happen  if  Perry  Como  got  hold  of  some  Big  Bill  Broonzy  material-but  it  grabbed  me  and  shook  me  up  like  a  religious  vision,  a  calling.  This  was  mainly  because  the  group  was  from  Ken-  tucky.  The  Hilltoppers  were  students  at  Western  [REDACTED]  State  College,  in  Bowling  Green,  where  the  sports  teams  were  called  theHilltoppers.  A  [REDACTED]  singing  quartet  had  achieved  national  fame! I  Love  " b"You.&quot;  With  the  arrival  of  rock  and  roll,  they  started  recording  livelier  im-  itations  of  black  tunes-&quot;'The  Door  Is  Still  Open,&quot;  &quot;Only  You,&quot;  and  Ruth  Brown's  &quot;Teardrops  from  My  Eyes.&quot;  On  some  of  the  songs  you  could  hear  a  rock-and-roll  saxophone  ora  boogie  piano,  and  even  a  bass  vocal  &quot;bum-  bum-bum&quot;  against  the  &quot;do-do-do-  do-do-do-do&quot;  background  harmony.
