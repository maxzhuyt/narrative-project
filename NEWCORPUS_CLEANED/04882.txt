I  think  it's  probably  the  most  beau-  tiful  mountain  I've  ever  seen.&quot;  &quot;'Thank  you,&quot;he  said,  as  if  the  mountain  were  his.  He  stood  there  in  the  sun,  ready  to  go.  But  for  the  first  time  he  seemed  all  at  once  unsure.  &quot;Please-&quot;'  he  said.  She  looked  up  at  him  anxiously.  &quot;Please,  you  will  not  to  tell  my  mother?&quot;  &quot;Oh,  of  course  not!&quot;  she  exclaimed.  Her  voice  simply  flew  out.  He  said  goodbye  then,  with  his  polite,  quick  bow,  and  went  off  down  the  path  and  out  of  sight.  After  a  moment  or  two,  Evelina  picked  up  her  glass,  finished  her  milk,  and  then  went  around  the  house  to  the  shaded  terrace.  &quot;Hi!&quot;  she  said  cheerful-  ly  to  her  mother  and  the  other  married  ladies.
