The  girl  was  reallyupset;  obvi-  ously  she  wasn't  as  tough  as  she  hoped  to  appear.  I  wanted  to  tell  her  the  man  had  hit  me,  too,  but  she  looked  too  angry.  The  man  must  have  been  one  of  those  pathological  people  I  had  previ-  ously  only  read  about,  like  the  man  who  enjoyed  stomping.  on  women's  in-  steps.  I  pictured  him  trudging  through  a  crowd  at  Grand  Central  Terminal,  casually  hitting  one  woman  after  the  next  with  the  hard  edge  of  his  attach\xc3\xa9  case.  Such  random  rage  made  no  sense  to  me,  yet  maybe  this  man  was  follow-  ing  some  natural  law.  Who  was  Ito  have  less  faith  than  people  who  had  studied  such  things  for  many  years?  When  I  got  to  Wilfredo's  neigh-  borhood,  there  was  a  light  drizzle.  I  hung  out  on  the  street  for  a  while,  watching  the  faces  as  they  came  along.  Everyone  had  the  same  features-two  eyes,  a  nose,  and  a  mouth-yet  every  face  was  slightly  different  from  the  next.  I  didn't  see  any  two  alike,  though  each  appeared  to  be  set  in  rec-  ognizable  expressions-despair,  fa-  tigue,  joy,  ennui.  I  think  I  could  have  stood  for  hours  watching  thepeople  go  by.  The  urgency  I'd  felt  was  now  diminished.  The  faces  seemed  a  partial  answer  to  a  question  I  couldn't  even  articulate.  But  after  a  while  it  started  to  rain  harder,  and  I  went  into  a  bookstore.  I  bought  four  books:  Dale  Carnegie's  &quot;How  to  Stop  Worrying  and  Start  Living,&quot;  a  book  on  transac-  tional  analysis  telling  how  to  write  your  own  life  script,  &quot;How  to  Find  a  Husband  in  Thirty  Days,&quot;  and  some-  thing  on  reincarnation.  The  four  books  cost  me  almost  thirty  dollars,  and  I  was  going  to  wait  until  I  got  home  to  look  at  them,  but  as  soon  as  I  hit  the  street  I  ripped  open  the  bag  and  pulled  one  out,  starting  to  read  asI  walked  to  the  subway  in  the  rain."
