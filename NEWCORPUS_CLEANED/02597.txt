She  wore  a  trenchcoat  and  carried  a  bag  of  groceries,  She  was  shorter  than  Nichola-not  really  much  like  her  at  all-but  I  thought  thatI  might  see  Nichola,  She  would  be  walking  along  some  street  maybe  not  far  from  here-  burdened,  preoccupied,  alone.  She  was  one  ofthe  grownup  people  in  the  world  now,  one  of  the  shoppers  go-  ing  home.  If  I  did  see  her,  I  might  just  sit  and  watch,  I  decided.  I  felt  like  one  of  those  people  who  have  floated  up  to  the  ceiling,  enjoying  a  brief  death.  A  re-  lief,  while  it  lasts,  My  father  had  chosen  and  Nichola  had  chosen.  Some-  day,  probably  soon,  I  would  hear  from  her,  but  it  came  to  the  same  thing.  I  meant  to  get  up  and  go  over  to  the  tomb,  to  look  at  the  relief  carvy-  ings,  the  stone  pictures,  that  go  all  the  way  around  it.  I  always  mean  to  look  at  them  and  I  never  do.  Not  this  time,  cither.  It  was  getting  cold  out,  so  I  went  inside  to  have  coffee  and  some-  thing  to  eat  before  I  went  back  to  the  hospital. "
