He  put  a  cigar  in  his  mouth,  but  he  didn't  light  it.  &quot;Nothing's  new,&quot;  I  said.  &quot;What's  new  with  you?&quot;  George  shrugged.  Then  he  grinned.  He  was  going  very  fast  now.  Wind  buffeted  the  car  and  whistled  by  outside  the  windows.  He  was  driving  as  if  we  were  late  for  work.  But  we  weren't  late.  We  had  lots  of  time,  and  I  told  him  so.  Nevertheless,  he  cranked  it  up.  We  passed  the  turnoff  and  kept  going.  We  were  moving  by  then,  heading  straight  toward  the  mountains.  He  took  the  cigar  out  of  his  mouth  and  put  it  in  his  shirt  pocket.  &quot;I  borrowed  some  money  andhad  this  baby  overhauled,&quot;  he  said.  Then  he  said  he  wanted  me  to  see  something.  He  punched  it  and  gave  it  everything  he  could.  I  fastened  my  seat  belt  and  held  on.  &quot;Go,&quot;  I  said.  &quot;What  are  you  wait-  ing  for,  George?&quot;  And  that's  when  we  really  flew.
