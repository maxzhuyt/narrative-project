Angela  was  one  of  the  mainstays  of  St.  Hilary's.  An  older  girl,  approaching  fifteen,  she  already  had  a  bosom,  her  calves  bulged,  and  so  portly  was  she  behind  that  the  back  pleats  of  her  serge  tunic  fanned  open. The  wounded  and  the  woebegone,  the  tremulous  and  the  infirm,  she  sought  out  assiduously  and  with  a  dead  accuracy  of  aim.  Babies,  it  seemed  certain,  would  in  time  simply  stream  from  her.
