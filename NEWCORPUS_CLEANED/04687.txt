Keeping  his  money  in  a  bank  was  an  equally  outlandish  idea. After  he  had  counted  the  total  sum,  he  always  sorted  the  bills  according  to  their  denomina-  tions,  then  sorted  them  according  to  their  degree  of  newness,  putting  the  brand-new  ones  in  one  pile,  the  slightly  worn  ones  in  another,  and  theold  ones  in  a  third. At  the  same  time  every  afternoon,  he  rose  from  his  nap,  sat  down  in  the  rattan  chair  in  his  bedroom,  and  waited  for  Liu  Lung,  the  deaf  manservant,  to  bring  him  tea,  a  water  pipe,  and  the  four  local  Chinese  newspapers.
