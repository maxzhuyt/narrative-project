The  words  were  mostly  in  English,  because  only  scholars,  or  eccentrics,  or  minis-  ters  could  spell  in  Gaelic,  and  her  grandmother  had  been  a  crofter  wife  who  had  not  gone  beyond  the  Strath-  Ard  school.  In  school,  of  course,  they  had  been  made  to  speak  English-  Mary,  too.  The  few  Gaelic  words  in  the  list  were  spelled  as  they  were  spoken,  and  the  scientific  phrases  were  probably  all  wrong.  But  Mary  saw  that  the  quality  of  the  past  was  there:  the  crofting  life  had  once  been  associated  with  learning,  with  grace.  The  hand-  writing  was  a  slanted,  delicate  web.  Mary  hung  on  to  the  fluttering  paper  in  the  wind,  and  resigned  herself  to  keeping  it.
