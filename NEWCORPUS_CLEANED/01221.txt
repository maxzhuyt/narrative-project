She  was  a  blur  behind  the  screen,  a  big  whiteshape  in  a  flowery  gown.  She  shifted  from  foot  to  foot.  &quot;You're  supposed  to  have  feelings  for  me,&quot;  I  said.  She  went  back  to  her  bed,  sank  down,  and  pulled  the  covers  up  over  her  head.  She  didn't  move.  Cicadas  ratcheted.  A  car  went  by  on  the  other  side  of  the  house,  radio  booming  higher  and  then  lower  as  it  shifted  into  the  distance.  After  a  while  I  heard  their  footsteps  inside-first  Marie's,  light  and  uneven;  then  his,  heavier  and  trudging-and  seconds  later  the  front  screen  snapping  shut,  her  voice  calling  out  just  after  it.  For  a  moment  I  was  sure  he'd  find  me  out.  I  crouched  in  the  bushes  until  I  saw  him  go  by  on  the  sidewalk,  strutting,  his  lips  pursed,  whistling-happily  oblivious.  Even  after  he  was  gone,  I  stayed  down  awhile,  not  sure  if  Marie  would  remain  inside  or  go  into  the  back  yard  for  a  smoke.  I  heard  sheets  moving  on  the  other  side  of  the  screen-Sarah  sighing,  the  bed  creaking-then  nothing.  My  knees  felt  like  they  would  pop  from  squatting,  so  I  stepped  out  of  the  bushes  and  turned  around.  What  I  saw  when  I  looked  back  in-  side  the  window  was  so  pretty-Sarah  and  Marie  curled  around  each  other,  front  to  back  in  the  bed,  their  hands  mov-  ing  over  each  other's  arms  and  shoulders,  one  turning  her  face  back  so  their  cheeks  were  almost  touching.  They  were  like  an  underwater  plant,  the  way  their  arms  and  legs  moved,  everything  about  them  matching.  And  after  a  minute  their  hands  came  together  on  the  stomach  of  the  one  who  lay  with  her  back  to  the  other,  and  their  bodies  began  falling  into  the  breathing  rhythm  of  sleep.  One  sister's  hair  lay  over  the  other's  neck.  One  pair  of  feet  were  bare,  the  other  had  on  blue  socks.  I  tried  to  remember  what  it  had  been  like  when  I  was  with  Marie.  I  closed  my  eyes  and  all  I  saw  was  houses.  I  saw  ladders  against  walls,  and  windows  with  no  outside  fixtures,  glass  taped  and  ledges  ready  for  paint.  I  saw  Marie's  house,  too,  newly  painted-  so  crisp  in  its  fresh  colors  you'd  never  guess,  underneath,  how  scaly  and  worn  to  splinters  the  boards  were."
