 THE  LAMENTATIONS  OF  NEO~JEREMIAH  OW  doth  the  city  cram  itself  H  with  people,  that  was  full  of  pleasant  places!  how  is  she  become  as  an  urban  redevelopment  center!  she  that  was  great  among  the  nations  and  princess  among  the  provinces,  where  are  now  her  brown-  stones  and  where  her  joyful  neighbor-  hoods?  2  The  builder  hath  swallowed  up  all  the  habitations  of  this  city,  and  hath  not  pitied;  he  hath  thrown  down  in  his  greed  the  strong  holds  of  beauty;  he  hath  brought  them  to  the  ground.  3  He  hath  swung  his  wrecking  ball  like  an  enemy;  he  stood  with  his  writ  of  eviction  as  an  adversary,  and  dismantled  all  that  was  pleasant  to  the  eye  in  the  tabernacle  of  the  daughter  of  my  peo-  ple;  he  replaced  it  with  stamped  alu-  minum.  4  He  hath  inclosed  our  ways  with  hewn  stone;  he  hath  planted  develop-  ments  across  the  side  streets,  that  no  man  may  pass  through:  he  doeth  it  for  universities  and  for  them  that  build  concert  halls,  yea,  for  any  committee  that  asketh.  5  All  her  people  sigh,  they  seek  sub-  urbs;  they  have  given  their  pleasant  things  for  a  patch  of  Scarsdale;  they  are  become  vile.  6  Zion  hath  grievously  sinned;  therefore  she  is  removed:  all  that  hon-  ored  her  despise  her,  because  they  have  seen  her  new  buildings;  yea,  they  gaze  at  Penn  Station  and  laugh.  7  All  that  pass  by  clap  their  hands  at  thee;  they  hiss  and  wag  their  heads        at  the  daughter  of  my  people,  saying,  Is  this  the  city  that  men  call  The  perfec-  tion  of  beauty,  The  joy  of  the  whole  earth?  8  The  elders  and  the  princes  sit  up-  on  the  ground  and  keep  silence;  all  summer  the  fountains  were  dry;  but  though  the  reservoirs  should  fail  utter-  ly,  yet  would  none  of  them  install  meters.  9  Thy  prophets  and  city  planners  have  seen  vain  and  foolish  things  for  thee;  Staten  Island  they  saved  not,  but  broke  the  ramparts  thereof;  they  have  seen  for  thee  false  burdens  and  causes  of  ugliness.  10  The  subways  did  not  run,  and  the  omnibuses  that  carried  people  stood  idle;  the  lights  failed;  the  papers  did  not  print;  all  Zion's  friends  have  dealt  treacherously  with  her;  they  arebe-  come  her  enemies.  11  For  these  things  I  weep:  mine  eye,  mine  eye  runneth  down  with  water;  my  liver  is  poured  upon  the  earth;  O  let  the  people  ask,  Who  shall  save  Zion  from  herself?
