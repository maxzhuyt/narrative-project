An Enduring Friendship-Flower and Snow

There's a conversation taking place just below the cold snow sprinkled floor of the forest.

“Wildflower, we've all decided that you can be the first to welcome spring this year.”

“Oh, can I mama? Can I?”

“Yes, my darling Flower. You've always had to follow after your siblings. But you're ready. You will make us proud. Your daddy and brothers and sisters will follow after you. It may be a day or two but fear none my love, we will be there. I will be the last to leave. It is my job to make sure everyone is up and out before I make my appearance. Don't be frightened you will have many extended family and friends watching over you until we arrive.

“I know mama. I'm never afraid. I look forward to sprouting and becoming new again.”

With those words Flower pushed upward with all her strength. She's a little wobbly but suddenly she sees a glimmer of the world.

There's another conversation taking place under a budding silver maple tree upon a small patch of existing snow.

“Hey Snowflake, are you ready yet. We're all waiting for you. Some of us can't wait too much longer,” shouts Whitie eagerly.

“Whitie, go tell mom to go without me.”

“Snowflake, I heard that young lady. Stop with that nonsense and come along. We haven't much time.”

“But mom, I'm not ready yet!”

“Do as your mother says Snow,” scolds her father.

As the sun's rays heat up the ground her family quickly departs. She hears their trailing words, “hurry Snow!”

Suddenly Snowflake is left all alone. Or she thought. She doesn't see the little pinkish flower peeking thru the snow that lingers on the ground.

“Hi.” Wildflower announces her presence in a tiny voice.

Snowflake looks around but doesn't see anyone or anything.

“Over here by the tree.”

Snowflake turns on point and sees her.

“Hi, what's your name?” She asks the pink flower.

“My name is Wildflower, but you can call me Flower.”

“That's a pretty name. OK, Flower. My name is Snowflake and you can call me Snow.”

From that day forward their friendship blossomed.

“So, what is life like as a flower, Flower?” Ha, Ha, Snow chuckles. Thinking it was funny to say flower Flower.

“Well, Snow it's wonderful. We get to live underground all winter long. My family and friends and I get to play games, sing and sleep. It's a different world down below. We wait until Mr. Groundhog says its Spring and then we all begin preparing to 'sprout'.”

“What do you mean by 'sprout'?” Inquires Snow.

“After we all stop rolling around and playing, we plant ourselves firmly in the soil. Then this wonderful feeling comes over us as we stand firm and tall. We take a deep breath and begin stretching. Suddenly we pop thru the ground. The first feel of the sun's warmth warms us and brings us great joy. But that's not all.”

“That sounds wonderful Flower! What else could there be?”

“Oh, the best part Snow.”

“The best part? What's that?” Questions Snow.

“Love.” Flower softly whispers.

“Love?”

“Yes, Love.”

“What do you mean?” Asked Snow.

“Well, humans know they're not supposed to pick us, but they do. Some come and pick lots of us at one time. They say they're taking us home for a bouquet. I don't know what that is, but it must be pretty special if they want many of us not just one. I can tell you some stories of things that happen to us Snow, if you want to hear them?”

“Oh, yes would you please?” Snow pleaded.

“Sure. One day a sweet elderly couple walking arm in arm were admiring my colorful friends. “Aren't those spring flowers the most beautiful you've ever seen?” The old woman said to the man. “Yes, but not as beautiful as you.” And with those words he bent down and picked one of my friends. He handed it to the old woman and gently kissed her forehead while softly whispering “I love you.” Another day a young couple walking hand in hand laughing and swinging their arms forward and back stopped a few feet from me. They both reached down, picked a flower and gave it to each other. But the very very best thing happened one beautiful sunny day.”

“What happened Snow?”

“A grandma and her granddaughter were strolling along when the little girl with shiny yellow boots began running between all of us. She was so careful as to not step on any flowers. Then unexpectedly she stopped right in front of me. My heart started beating so fast. Pick me. Pick me. I prayed. I beamed with anticipation as she slowly bent over and a little hand gently gripped my stem and slowly pulled me from my hold. I was so happy. The little girl smiled as she handed me to her grandma.”

“We all want to be picked Snow. We treasure the adventure that awaits us. That's what we grow for. To share our beauty and to make others happy. As you can see our colors spread like a blanket across the still white landscape but soon, we will shine brightly upon the vast forest floor along with other Spring foliage. As Spring transfers to Summer some of us, if we're not picked that is, began to depart. We go back to our underground homes only to reappear next Spring. It's a wonderful life Snow. I wouldn't trade it for anything.”

“Oh Flower, that sounds like an incredible life.”

“It truly is, Snow. So, what's your story. How is life for you?”

“Well, Flower, my life is pretty spectacular too!”

“Oh, tell me! Tell me!” Cried Flower.

“OK, our lives begin in the sky.”

“Wow! Way up there?” Flower looks straight up.

“Yes. Do you see those white fluffy things in the sky?”

“Ya, I do.”

“Those are clouds. We get to play on them. They're soft and so much fun. We sink in them but float right back out. Floating from cloud to cloud can get pretty tiring. Sometimes my other special friends have to leave the clouds and fall through the sky.”

“What happens to your special friends.”

“Well, they're different than me. I'm 'snow'. They are called 'rain'. The temperature has to be just right for them. Not to hot and not to cold. They fall to the ground and get everything wet. If they fall where there's sand or dirt, they make puddles. And humans seem to love puddles.”

“I know what you are talking about Snow. Your friends rein down on us. When we get hot, they help cool us off and help us to grow!”

“Ya Flower, my rain friends can be very helpful.”

“My snow friends and family wait a long time to be born, just like you. All Spring, Summer and Fall we play games, sing, float from cloud to cloud and sleep. As you wait for Mr. Groundhog we wait for Old Man Winter. When he announces his arrival, we are ready. The temperature is just right, and we are all so excited. We begin romping, laughing and spinning around. Suddenly we are free falling from the sky. It's so much fun. Oh, we land on the ground. Many of us land on top of each other then we're whisked away by Mr. Wind only to find our way back. There are so many of us. We're impossible to count. We are all different but yet we are the same.”

“What do you mean by that Snow? How can you be the same and different?”

“Good question Flower. We're the same because we all have six points or sides.” She showed her friend her points, which were now a little more round then pointy. “But we all look different because our bodies are unique.” She continued.

“So, your friends look different from you?”

“Yup.”

“What do you and your friends do?” Inquired Flower.

“Humans love to play with us! They like to roll us in different size snowballs. And let me tell you, it sure makes me dizzy. Then they stack us together and make what they call a 'snowman'. They put funny things on us. Sometimes it tickles. Some of them like to make snow forts. The young kids make more snowballs and throw them at one another. We go whizzing past each other. Sometimes we collide together. Oh man that is so much fun. We sort of crumble to the ground. It's really exhilarating when lots of us get together and make a snow hill. The people enjoy sledding or skiing down us. We need to make sure we're lying on our bellies before they do, or it really hurts. Sometimes we get our points knocked off but that's OK. We hang out and play with the humans for a very long time. We are devoted to pleasing them. But not all humans are happy to see us.”

“Why wouldn't they be happy. It sounds like a lot of enjoyment.”

“We are not all fun and play. Some of us have the job of being not so nice.”

“What do you mean?”

“Well, some of my friends land on cars. Humans don't like when they have to brush us off. Or when we land on roads, driveways and sidewalks. That makes it dangerous for them, they're not happy. Because there's so many of us it takes a lot of work to move us. But we are determined. Just like you and your friends Flower, we keep coming back every year.

“Wow! That all sounds amazing!” Flower excitedly admitted.

“Snow!” yells a familiar voice.

“Yes, mama!” Snow response.

“Snow, you need to come now. Our time is over. Come now Snnnooooowww!”

The voice trails off in the distance.

“Flower, I must go.”

“I know Snow.” It's Spring! This life here ends for you, and new life here begins for me.”

“But we will meet again at this same place next year. We will play, sing and dance! And spend lots more time together.” Cries Snow as she slowly departs.

“I love you, Flower!” were the last words Flower heard from her new founded friend.

“I love you too Snow!”

Flower stood proud and contented. After all it was the beginning of Spring. She was the first to sprout. The sun wrapped her in its heavenly warmth. New life begins. And the start of a new friendship with Snow. What more could she ask for. Nothing.
