M25 Aivie and Arman At The Spring of the World

Aivie was sitting in the middle of the most beautiful bunch of spring flowers that she had ever seen. The gentle breeze accentuated their marvelous aroma, rather than disperse it. The smell, the vibrant combinations of yellow, blue, red and several other less prevalent colors, the bees flitting between them, all made Aivie so happy.

A butterfly landed on her forehead and lazily pumped its wings. At first, she was amazed as her eyes crossed, trying to look up at it. But then, some of her ever-present melancholy crept into her thought again.

“Even a butterfly thinks my huge forehead the best place to land.”

She slowly raised her left hand to her face and then slid it up higher. The butterfly did not take off, choosing instead to step nimbly over her fingers to stride about. Aivie brought her hand back down slowly so as not to startle her visitor, watching its light movements. It was so colorful and pretty.

It fought the light breeze for a moment that pressed on its wings, then gave in and took flight again. Aivie’s hand raised slightly to follow the butterfly as it left her.

“Be happy gentle soul,” she said as her recent friend flew away. “I wasn’t sad because of you. I only wish I knew why I had to be so different.”

Her statement crossed the unseen pathways of the ethereal realm, reaching the ears of someone who had waited many years for such an opportunity.

A handsome, but much older male appeared before her. He seemed to stand straighter and taller than most. His forehead was much like hers. She could not tell if he was glowing or if it was a trick of the sunlight from behind him.

“I have been waiting years to talk to you, my child.”

“You asked why you are different. You are not just different. You are better. The day will come when most people look like you and very few will be like the others.”

“You will meet a handsome male warrior whose forehead is like yours one day. Your offspring will change the world. Until then, wait and be happy.”

With that advice, the stranger became enveloped in a bright light and vanished, leaving Aivie to wonder if it had really happened.

- - -

And so it came to pass that Aivie grew into adulthood and took her place with the single women of the clan. On this particular sunny spring day, she was in the center of camp helping to work grain in the grinding stone. It was tiresome work but left her mind free to wander.

A commotion started up and got louder as a group approached the center of the clan area where Aivie worked. When the group stopped in front of her, and she saw the handsome, well-muscled man in the middle who was obviously the cause of the hubbub, she stood up.

He looked directly at Aivie and spoke to her, “My name is Arman. I have been looking for you.”

“Me? How did you know I was here?”

“A stranger told me that the most beautiful girl in the twelve clans lived by this stream.”

Aivie blushed darker than she thought was possible. No one had ever said that she was pretty, much less beautiful.

The other women were obviously envious of Aivie. True the stranger had a bulge in his forehead also, but he was soooo virile and handsome. How was it possible that he was only interested in the ugly girl!

And so, as the world continued to come alive with spring, so did the bond between Aivie and Arman grow until they decided to start a family unit of their own.

- - -

In the course of time…

Aivie sat by the river the following spring. She was supposed to be getting water in her bucket to take back to her hut, but she needed to rest a moment. The life growing in her belly was nearly ready to come out, and it tired her so. She decided to take a moment and just take in the beauty of the world waking up again. Flowers and the insects tending them were everywhere.

As she sat cupping her belly with both hands, humming softly to the not yet born child, she mused out loud, "Life has been kind to me, and it will be kind to you, my baby."

The glow from behind alerted her to the fact that she was no longer alone.

"Aivie. You are soon to be the mother of the child who will change the world."

She turned to see the older wise man whom she had seen so many years before standing behind her. He looked much as she remembered him, very old, very well groomed, very serene.

"It's been so long since you were here," she said.

"Yes, I have watched but not come."

"Why do you say my child will change everything?"

"The child of Aivie and Arman will be even more beautiful than you. She will be known in times yet to come as Lucy, though you will probably give her a different name. Her children will travel far. Just as life now springs from the world, so too will the life that springs from you grow strong and multiply."

As the stranger started to fade again, he said, "Your daughter will be everyone's grandmother. She will become the Spring of the World."
