Facilitators Save Easter

The 143rd holder of the title of The Easter Bunny was worried. His ears drooped as he nodded his head and listened to the medical bunny explain why so many of his helper bunnies were sick.

Things did not look good in Bunnyville. How was he ever to get ready for Easter in time. It was less than a week away!

“It’s an epidemic,” the medical bunny was saying. “All these bunnies will be sick for several days and unable to work for some days after that. And then, of course, there will be more falling sick as well. I don’t know where we are going to put them all!”

“That may be the least of our problems,” the Easter Bunny said. “If we miss Easter, we may never be believed again!”

Under the bed next to which the two discussed their situation, Jenisey, the pixie looked over at her friend Hohense, the fairy.

“We have to DO something!” Jenisey said as she shifted her weight from one foot to the other and back.

“But what?! We can’t make everyone better,” Hohense replied, his wings stiff and quivering behind him.

“Let’s call a meeting of the Faye and see if anyone has any ideas,” Jenisey decided.

The pixie and fairy flew silently from under the bed and out a nearby window.

- - -

The Faye gathered at the Grand Lodge when matters of import were to be discussed. It was a large space in a hollow tree with enough room to accommodate everyone. Acorns were arranged about the space for seating and a bower of decorative twigs and leaves served as a lectern for the speaker.

Jenisey and Hohense were at the lectern this day as pixies, sprites, elves and fairies spread out before them.

“Friends, Faye, Countrymen,” Jenisey began. “Sorry, I couldn’t resist the misquote!”

“But seriously, there is a crisis in the realm of mythical creatures, and I believe that we can help,” she continued.

There were quiet rumblings from the assembled Faye but none loud enough to be so impolite as to interrupt.

Hohense spoke up to add, “Our good friend the Easter Bunny has a massive problem!” This caused several intakes of breath and focused the attention of the room more intensely.

Hohense turned to Jenisey holding out his hand, palm up, indicating that she should continue.

“It’s true! The Easter Bunny has a shortage of helpers this year because of the flu. I’m sure that everybody would love to help color the eggs, but we all know that we are just not big enough to handle the heavy eggs successfully.”

There were many nods and words of agreement.

“So, Hohense and I have called this meeting to see if anyone would have an idea about how we might help.”

There was a bit of murmuring and heads were put together and then finally Starmello, a sprite stood up and said, “I might know just the one who can help.”

“Go on.”

“I recently met a little girl named Sally. She is an orphan. who lives at the Children’s Home of St. Mary’s in town.”

“I’m sure every extra hand will help, but I think we need more than just one girl,” Hohense said solemnly.

“Oh, I agree,” Starmello said. “You see, I think Sally has many friends who would also help. The only problem is that we would have to let them know we exist and then count on them to keep our secret.”

“I say we try it,” someone in the rear shouted and many voices added agreement.

“I don’t think we have a choice,” Jenisey agreed. “When can you ask her?”

“I‘ll talk to her tonight!”

“Do it! I’ll go tell the Easter Bunny,” Hohense said.

- - -

Sally was sitting on one of the beds lined up in two rows in the dorm of the orphanage that she shared with twenty other girls. She had a book open on her lap but was having trouble concentrating on reading when Starmello hopped up and perched on the top edge of it.

“What are you doing, Starmello!” Sally hissed as she tried to turn to hide her visitor. “Everyone will see you!”

“Do you think your friends can be trusted?” Starmello asked.

“Yes, but you said we should keep you a secret,” she replied in a whisper.

“Times change. Something important has come up. We need you and all your friends to help us.”

Fitting action to his words, the little sprite leapt from the book and bounced around the room, hopping on heads, shoulders and outstretched hands, nimbly avoiding being caught. All the girls started pointing and as the volume of giggling and laughing increased, Sally shushed them.

When Starmello came back to her and settled on her shoulder, she said, “Everyone gather around. We can’t let the headmistress hear us! This is my friend Starmello. He needs our help.”

Twenty anxious faces pressed in around Sally and Starmello.

“I know that I can count on all of you,” Starmello began. “Most of you probably know that Easter is not far away.”

Heads nodded in agreement around the circle of wide-eyed girls.

“What you don’t know is that the Easter Bunny has a problem and needs your help.”

A chorus of questions and exclamations erupted from the girls. “What’s wrong? The EASTER Bunny! What can WE do?”

“Quiet down!” Starmello hissed, looking about. “The helper bunnies are all sick and can’t get the eggs colored in time. The Easter Bunny needs all of you to do it.”

“I know! We can work in the basement. No one will find out,” Sally said.

“You will have to work at night. You will not get much sleep,” Starmello warned.

There was a milder hubbub, but agreement was quickly reached. The girls of St Mary’s would get the job done!

“The Easter Bunny will bring the eggs, all of you will color them, then the bunnies will take them back to Bunnyville. A couple of nights should do it.”

“When do we start?” someone asked.

“The Easter Bunny has the first batch of eggs waiting outside. We start right now!”

“We are going to meet the Easter Bunny?” Sally asked.

“Yes, but we have to move quickly and quietly,” Starmello answered.

The excited girls filed out of the dorm and made their way as silently as little girls could down to the basement. The delivery door was opened and the Easter Bunny introduced himself. Behind him stood three carts, pulled by teams of four large rabbits, each filled with boxes of eggs.

The girls cleared out a workspace, moving boxes and the miscellaneous flotsam of many years, which had been discarded in the cellar but was not yet ready to be thrown away, to new locations against the walls. The bare lightbulbs suspended from the ceiling did not bathe the room in bright light, but they did make it light enough to see by.

No time was wasted. Sally organized the girls into several assembly lines and some transport groups. The few helper bunnies quickly set up the coloring bowls and drying racks as the transport groups distributed eggs to the beginning of each coloring line.

Hundreds of eggs were colored and hauled away on the carts that first night.

This work continued for two more nights. The girls had continued with their normal lives during the day, and then they crept down to the basement at night and colored egg after egg until they could no longer keep their eyes open.

Finally, the Easter Bunny declared that the quota had been reached

“I don’t know how I can ever thank you all!” the Easter Bunny said. “You have saved Easter for the town!”

The exhausted but happy team of girls made their way to their beds to dreams of bunnies and colorful eggs and happy times.

- - -

The Easter Bunny made his usual rounds that Easter. Neither the children of the town nor their parents ever knew the role that the girls of the Children’s Home of St. Mary’s played in making that possible. But twenty little girls knew, and they grew up with the knowledge that doing good for good’s sake is reward enough.
